About Nick

I am a researcher at Washington State University's Measure to Manage project, where I manage the entire technical stack, engaging in activities that range from data munging to statistical analysis and data visualization, SQL database to web development, article authoring to publication-quality figure creation. I work with (in descending order) python, javascript, python, stata, d3, julia, and a smattering of other languages and tools.

My interests are broad and fall into a few large categories:

  • Statistics and prediction
  • Machine Learning and other 'automated' methods
  • Data visualization and interaction
  • Agriculture as it pertains to food production and methods, food security and famine, and nutrition
  • Economics of competition, intellectual property, and monopoly power

While my education and experience is largely as an economist and mathematician, from 2008 to 2011 I was in West Africa with the Peace Corps, where I saw first hand the importance of agriculture for local populations and local economies. I think there is substantial room for the application of statistical methods to questions of food security and food production methods and how they affect local economies.

I'm a believer in open source software, open data, collaboration, and the 'gift economy'. In my non-existant spare time I write fiction and get outside as much as possible.
