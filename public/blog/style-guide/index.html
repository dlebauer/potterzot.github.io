Building a Style Guide for Non-Designers

Aug 25, 2015

There is something about style guides that feel integrated, tidy, and just plain clean to me. Maybe this is because I imagine that putting together graphics is just a whole lot easier when all of the font family, font size, line weights, and colors have been already decided.

And a style guide doesn't just help with graphics, it also makes document creation of all sorts easier, and everything that you output shares a consistent look and feel. There's one word that captures this, and that word is "polished."

Anyway, I've been wanting to make my own style guide for a while, but my strength is with statistics and programming, not design. But by following some guides and themes, I've managed to come up with a style guide that I quite like, and from what I can tell there are a few important design principals to follow (though again, not my expertise):

  • Header text should be a serif font (or opposite of the text)
  • Basic text should be a sans-serif font
